Remote delivery?

Office location and office hours should be through Zoom or Google Meet if your course will be offered through remote delivery.

Accessible office hours

Include, if possible, the option to meet remotely via Google Hangouts or Zoom, or to schedule meetings at more flexible times using Google Calendar. Consider giving students the option of group office hours rather than individual meetings. Meeting virtually and in flexible time slots makes office hours more accessible to students who commute long distances, have family or work commitments, or disabilities that make it difficult for them to be on campus or meet at specific times. Group meetings can make office hours less intimidating to students who are shy, anxious, or struggling with the material. Learn how to use Google Calendar to create appointment slots for office hours: Teaching with Google Apps > Using Appointment Slots

Phone

If your course is offered through remote delivery, we leave it to your discretion if you wish to provide a phone number or if you prefer to only use email.

Lecture tips

Lecturing can be an effective way to transfer information, but students learn best when the lecture includes some active components. If you would like to learn more about making lectures more engaging, please refer to: Teaching Strategies

Case method tips

In case method, students are typically given a real-life problem to solve. Case method is active, experiential, and often features group work or collaboration. To learn more about case methods, refer to: Teaching Strategies

Debates and presentation tips

Class debates and oral presentations are often part of group work. Group work can be very effective and assistive to students, but requires considerable planning. If you would like to learn more about effective group work, please refer to: Teaching Strategies

Combination of teaching methods

Try to use a variety of instructional methods when presenting materials to your students. This assists all students in understanding material and keeps your course active. If you would like to learn about course delivery tips that make a difference, please refer to: Teaching Resources

Course materials tips

Course materials can be inaccessible and costly for many of our students. The Library is dedicated to ensuring your course materials are accessible to your students, providing online course readings for your D2L course and accessible formats and captioned films for students with disabilities. The Library can also help you find free open educational readings and textbooks for your course.

Equipment use

Consider telling your students why the textbook and or equipment/materials were selected and where they can be purchased or accessed. If you have a course policy regarding the use of electronic devices in class, it is a good idea to state this as part of the Course Overview.

Instructional technology requirements

Students appreciate when they can easily access the technology requirements for your course.

Learning outcome tips

Learning outcomes identify the various specific pieces that go into the cultivation of the knowledge, skills and attitudes the program is intended to develop. Course learning objectives are the result or outcome of program learning experiences. For assistance with developing learning outcomes for your course, please check out tips available on Best Practices in Course Design or book an individual consultation.

D2L calendar tip

Consider using the D2L course calendar tools to specify and reinforce due dates, course events and activities. Visit Brightspace Help > Instructor > Course Planning > Calendar.

For remote teaching let students know how they should be managing their time each week. You can even provide them with a table estimating their time commitment for each area of the course.

Accessible tables

Make sure tables are easy to navigate with assistive technologies.
  • Avoid merging cells that span multiple columns or rows.
  • Add column headings and indicate a header row.
  • Ensure the table has good contrast.
  • Add a table description for more complex tables.
  • Avoid colour coding. Use a combination of text, shapes, and colour instead.

Watch a 2 minute video on how to create accessible tables in Microsoft Word.
